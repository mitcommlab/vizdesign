Gestalt's Principles

Visual Hierarchy

Highlights

  • Visual Load:
    • Color themes are strategically chosen and used while retaining connectedness, similarity and enclosure
    • White background used to lighten visual load while retaining emphasis on features (Figure-ground)

  • Visual Flow:
    • Circular flow (closure) coupled with feature alignment, arrow connections and similarity direct reader’s line of sight
    • Arrows are drawn with similar size and relative directions, indicating common fate of the influence of the field by itself and on the following field (e.g. Biology → Technology)
    • Similar sizes, colors and contrast were chosen to convey equal weighting of each field
    • Repetition and proximity relate features to their respective bubbles and fields
    • Fonts and features have varying textures and styles to guide line of sight

Proximity

  • Some of the lines are closer to each other, while others not, giving a sense of the extent of their inter-relationship

  • Different levels of proximity overall indicate some notion of complexity, which relates to "Computation"

Similarity

  • Features within each individual third are colored similarly to indicate belonging to the same field

  • Features are also of similar shapes and patterns, allowing the reader to infer that they belong to the same field

Enclosure

  • Circles around the field, along with the colored dividers indicate that the fields are segmented into Biology, Computation and Technology

Common Fate

  • Inferences from field (in text) follow a similar fate:
    • Outward (impact on the field)
    • Inward/Around the circle (impact on the next field)

  • Arrows are of similar lengths, relative direction and curvature

Closure

  • Despite the absence of an actual circular boundary, arrangement of features leads the reader to perceive that a circular boundary exists.

Continuity

  • Gradation in circle colors are perceived as depth and related to each other

  • Use of color to emphasize the continuous connections between circles of interest

Connection

  • Arrows direct visual flow in a circular fashion

  • Demonstrates relationships between each field

Figure & Ground

  • Left: Use of white background within the bubbles and bubbles being in front of the core circle draws attention to words, arrows and circle borders

  • Right: Use of white background within the core circle draws attention to the features within each third

Size

  • Bubbles corresponding to each field have similar sizes, which reflect equal importance

  • Circle thirds are also similarly sized to avoid drawing attention away from each other to convey equal weighting

  • Large size of the central circle draws attention to the features within before the bubbles

Color

  • "Biology" has a marginally darker color compared to "Technology" and "Computation", which draws the reader's eyes to the top of the circle to start the circular visual path. Since the target audience has expertise in Systems Biology, the familiarity of "Biology" would also aid this hierarchy.

  • Each field is represented by its own color, which relates and groups its respective features

Contrast

  • Features generally have similar levels of contrast to strengthen the notion of equal importance.

Alignment

  • Bubbles and arrows are aligned in a circular fashion to direct visual flow. (One would naturally move in the direction Biology → Technology → Computation)

Repetition

  • Within each circle third, features have repeated styles for consistency within each field and to strengthen field identity

  • Directions and sizes of arrows are repeated to show their related message of impact of the field and on the next field

Proximity

  • Features within the circle third are close to their respective bubbles, relating them to the field denoted in the bubble

Whitespace

  • Bubbles for each field use whitespace to draw attention to the center of the bubble, which states the field

Texture and Style

  • Size and capitalization styles of the words denoting the field draw attention and represent importance

  • Impact of field and on the next field are "supporting information" and have styles with smaller size and no capitalization. The marginally smaller size relative to the field names still allows readers to move their line of sight to them from field names

  • Field names and impacts have similar font styles to show relatedness and similar levels of performance
